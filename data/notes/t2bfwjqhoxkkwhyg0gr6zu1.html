Gauss Lemma

The crux of the proof that geodesics are locally minimizing is the following deceptively simple geometric lemma.

Theorem 6.9 (The Gauss Lemma). Let (M,g)(M, g)(M,g) be a Riemannian manifold, let UUU be a geodesic ball centered at p∈Mp \in Mp∈M, and let ∂r\partial_{r}∂r​ denote the radial vector field on U∖{p}U\setminus \{p\}U∖{p}. Then ∂r\partial_{r}∂r​ is a unit vector field orthogonal to the geodesic spheres in U∖{p}U\setminus \{p\}U∖{p}.
