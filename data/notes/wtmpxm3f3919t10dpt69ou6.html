General properties of groups of isometries in metric spaces

Definitions

Let GGG be a group of isometries in a metric space XXX, we say that the action of GGG is proper if the action map

ϕ:G×X→X×X,(g,x)↦(x,g.x),\phi: G\times X \to X \times X, \qquad (g, x) \mapsto (x, g.x),ϕ:G×X→X×X,(g,x)↦(x,g.x),

is a proper map (i.e. the preimage ϕ−1(C)\phi^{-1}(C)ϕ−1(C) of any compact set C⊂X×XC \subset X \times XC⊂X×X is compact). Equivalently, ϕ\phiϕ is proper if and only if, for any compact set K⊂XK \subset XK⊂X, the set

{g∈G∣g(K)∩K≠∅}\left\{ g \in G\mid g(K) \cap K \neq \emptyset \right\}{g∈G∣g(K)∩K=∅}

is compact. For discrete subgroups, this definition is equivalent to requiring that the set @eq-proper-action-equiv (Private) is finite, as in the definition of Scott @scott1983 (Private) pg. 86.

Discrete subgroups of isometries

The following proposition resumes the basic properties of groups of isometries acting on a metric space XXX, the tool for the proof will be Arzela-Ascoli theorem. Recall a family of continuous functions F\mathcal{F}F is equicontinuous if for all ϵ>0\epsilon > 0ϵ>0 exists δ>0\delta > 0δ>0 such that

Critical exponent of groups of isometries

Let Γ\GammaΓ be a discrete subgroup of isometries of the complete metric space XXX with the Heine-Borel property and let x,y∈Xx, y \in Xx,y∈X be any pair of points, we can form the Poincare series of the group, defined as,

P(s;x,y)=∑γ∈Γe−sd(x,γy),s∈R.P(s; x, y) = \sum_{\gamma \in \Gamma} e^{-s d(x, \gamma y)}, \qquad s \in \mathbb{R}.P(s;x,y)=γ∈Γ∑​e−sd(x,γy),s∈R.

The critical exponent of the group is the number δΓ\delta_\GammaδΓ​ such that the series is convergent for s>δΓs > \delta_\Gammas>δΓ​. It can be shown that δΓ\delta_\GammaδΓ​ is independent of the pair of points chosen in XXX, in fact, it is well known that

δΓ=lim sup⁡R→∞log⁡(#{γ∈Γ∣γx∈B(x,R)})R.\delta_\Gamma = \limsup_{R \to \infty} \frac{\log (\#\{\gamma \in \Gamma \mid \gamma x \in B(x, R)\})}{R}.δΓ​=R→∞limsup​Rlog(#{γ∈Γ∣γx∈B(x,R)})​.

If XXX is also endowed with a measure mmm, we can define the volume entropy of XXX as,

δX=lim sup⁡R→∞log⁡m(B(x,R))R.\delta_X = \limsup_{R \to \infty} \frac{\log m(B(x,R))}{R}.δX​=R→∞limsup​Rlogm(B(x,R))​.

The following facts are well known for manifolds of negative curvature, however they are valid for more general metric spaces.

The visual boundary in a metric space

Let (X,d)(X, d)(X,d) be a complete metric space, a geodesic in XXX is a continuous function γ:R→X\gamma: \mathbb{R} \to Xγ:R→X such that

d(γ(s),γ(t))=∣s−t∣.d(\gamma(s), \gamma(t)) = |s - t|.d(γ(s),γ(t))=∣s−t∣.
